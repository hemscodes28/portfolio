AI-Powered Train Traffic Control

Maximizing Section Throughput Using AI-Powered Precise Train Traffic Control is a conceptual AI-based approach proposed to improve railway traffic efficiency through predictive scheduling and safety-aware decision support. The solution was presented during the Smart India Hackathon internal round and recognized for its feasibility and system design.

Problem Statement

  • Railway networks experience frequent congestion, leading to delays and reduced operational efficiency
  • Traditional signaling systems lack the ability to dynamically optimize train spacing and scheduling in real time
  • Manual traffic control methods are largely reactive, causing cascading delays across interconnected routes
  • There is a need for intelligent, data-driven systems to maximize track utilization while maintaining strict safety protocols
  • Current systems lack predictive capabilities to identify and prevent bottlenecks before they occur

What Was Built

  • Designed an AI-powered conceptual framework to optimize train spacing and scheduling for maximum section throughput
  • Proposed machine learning-based analysis of historical railway traffic data to identify congestion-prone sections
  • Conceptualized a predictive decision-support system to assist railway traffic controllers (IRSCC / IRSTC integration)
  • Designed logic for delay prediction and proactive schedule adjustment recommendations
  • Created system architecture and workflow designs representing AI-assisted railway traffic control
  • Proposed a simulation-based evaluation approach to test multiple traffic scenarios and control strategies
  • Incorporated railway safety regulations and operational constraints into the system design
  • Designed a visualization dashboard concept for monitoring train positions, speeds, and predicted arrival times

Outcome & Recognition

  • Presented as a conceptual AI-based railway traffic control solution during the Smart India Hackathon internal round
  • Secured 3rd place at the department-level internal hackathon based on innovation and feasibility
  • Advanced through presentation and evaluation rounds focusing on system design and AI applicability
  • Project did not proceed to final national implementation, as it was limited to the presentation stage
